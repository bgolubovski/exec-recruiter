# Meeting prep recipes by round and interviewer altitude

This reference codifies the prep emphasis per interview round and per interviewer altitude. Use it to shape what the packet covers, what to compress, and what to expand.

## Universal sections (always present)

Every prep packet includes:

1. Header card with meeting context (who, when, format, channel that led to it, status)
2. The disambiguating question (varies by round + altitude)
3. Interviewer profile (background, career arc, education, signals to use, signals to avoid)
4. Founder / leadership context (especially if the interviewer reports to a named founder)
5. Company context (origin, pivots, funding, customers, anchor case studies, stage shape signals)
6. Pattern-match anchors in priority order (Vector and Compass kept distinct, per memory)
7. First-90-days narrative (one or three branched versions)
8. Questions to ask
9. One-line answers to likely questions back
10. Comp ask posture
11. Friction flags to listen for
12. Wrap-up signals to send by the call's last 5 minutes
13. Do nots
14. Logistics + pre-call self-check
15. Sources

## Round-specific emphasis

### `screen` (recruiter or hiring-manager intro, 30 min)

This is the most common first call. The "incumbent-product-leader screening a potentially-above-them candidate" case is the canonical archetype.

**Compress:**
- Background pitch to 60 seconds
- 90-day narrative to a single confident frame (or branched if scope is ambiguous)

**Expand:**
- The scope-disambiguation question and 1-2 backup variants
- Interviewer profile signals worth probing
- One pointed customer / domain question to ask
- Comp ask posture (defer-volunteering)

**Compress further (skip):**
- Reference-check prep
- Deep negotiation prep
- Board-readiness narrative

### `working-session` (2nd round, 60-90 min, often product / strategy deep-dive)

The interviewer will probe specific product trade-offs, customer reality, what good looks like at depth. Often a single product trade-off becomes the load-bearing exercise.

**Compress:**
- General "background pitch" (you've already covered this in screen)

**Expand:**
- 2-3 named customer / product trade-off scenarios with framing on how to think out loud
- Concrete examples from your career where you've made similar trade-offs (Vector concept-to-prod cycle decisions, Northstar multi-product portfolio kills, Helix scope-cut moments, Atlas pricing-and-packaging trade-offs)
- Technical fluency markers without overclaiming (Vector LLM-grounding patterns specifically, NOT general ML theory)

**Add new section:**
- "If they hand you a problem statement to think through" - decision rubric you can voice while solving live

### `panel` (multiple panelists, often back-to-back 30-min slots, 2-4 hours total)

**Compress:**
- Background pitch ONCE; assume panelists have read your CV before the panel

**Expand:**
- Per-panelist coverage map: who probes which dimension and what they're testing for (Eng panelist tests technical fluency, Sales panelist tests GTM judgment, CFO tests commercial-model literacy, etc.)
- A through-line across panelists (your top 2 anchors should appear in every panelist's conversation)
- Calibration signal: how each panelist's question hints at the role's actual ranking criteria

**Add new section:**
- "Common questions across panel; vary the example you use" - the same anchor told three different ways for three different panelists

### `final` (sometimes back-to-back with CEO + CFO or CEO + board observer)

**Compress:**
- Most prep work is done; the final is calibration not discovery

**Expand:**
- Board-readiness narrative (your IPO-prep work at Helix is the lead anchor)
- Commercial-model strategy posture (pricing, packaging, enterprise-vs-PLG split)
- Comp negotiation prep: hard floor, equity expectations, vesting cliff norms, sign-on stance, severance language to look for
- "What would make this hire a regret in 12 months?" - sharp question, sharp answer
- Reference-check prep: who you'd hand them, talking points per referee

### `exec` (CEO or founder, often standalone, often the second or final call)

**Compress:**
- Tactical product judgment (already covered earlier in process)

**Expand:**
- Strategic / commercial / fundraising narrative
- "How would you spend the next two years building this company?" depth
- Founder dynamic read: what kind of product partner do they actually want (the Dr. Lara Mendez / [research-PhD-turned-founder] read is the canonical example - first-principles thinker, hates MBA-framework dressing)
- Risk-and-conviction questions (where would you push back on me? What would you NOT do?)

**Add new section:**
- "Founder partnership stance" - explicit framing on how you operate alongside founder voice without bruising founder ownership

### `reference-check-prep` (not a meeting, but a meeting-adjacent moment)

**Compress:**
- Everything except the reference list curation

**Expand:**
- Reference list: 3-5 names with role, context, what they'd validate, why they're a good reference for THIS specific role
- Talking points per referee: 2-3 specific stories you'd want them to be primed to tell
- Heads-up note draft: short message to each referee giving them context on the role + what to expect

## Interviewer altitude modifiers

These modifiers tune the packet's tone and emphasis on top of the round above.

### Recruiter / Talent screener

- Tone: friendly-professional, low-ego, "make their job easy"
- Lead with: clarity on comp band, geo / work-auth, timeline, motivation
- Don't lead with: deep technical detail or scope debates - those are for the hiring manager
- Expected: they'll ask 5-8 standard questions, you should have crisp 60-second answers
- Decision criteria: do they think you're worth passing to the hiring manager? Be persuasive-but-not-pushy.

### Peer (sibling VP, peer Head of, fellow operator)

- Tone: peer-to-peer, operator-mindset, intellectually honest
- Lead with: what you'd actually do in the first 90 days as a peer
- Don't lead with: hierarchy or authority signals; peers test for collaboration not dominance
- Expected: real product trade-off questions, war stories about cross-functional conflict, working-style fit
- Decision criteria: do they want to work alongside you?

### Incumbent in the same seat (you're potentially replacing them or sitting above them)

- Tone: explicit respect for what they've built
- Lead with: a clean disambiguation of scope (above / beside / backfill) early, then the narrative branches based on the answer
- Don't lead with: anything that implies "I'd come fix what you got wrong"
- Expected: defensive-curious tone from them, signals about what they hope changes, signals about what they want preserved
- Decision criteria: do they think the org gets stronger with you in?

The Sam Carter case is the canonical example here.

### Hiring manager (1-2 levels above you)

- Tone: direct, decisive, candid
- Lead with: scope clarity, your conviction on the most important moves in the first 90 days, the friction flags you've already identified
- Don't lead with: hedging language, soft framing, false modesty
- Expected: they'll test for whether you can hold your own in the role, judgment under ambiguity, comp clarity
- Decision criteria: would they bet on you?

### Founder / CEO

- Tone: first-principles, intellectually rigorous, willing-to-disagree-cleanly
- Lead with: a sharp read on where you'd add most in the next 12 months
- Don't lead with: framework-heavy thinking dressed as insight; founders smell that fast
- Expected: high-variance questions, willingness to go off-script, signals about the founder's actual product instincts
- Decision criteria: would they trust you as a product partner?

### Board observer / investor (rare in first rounds, occasionally surfaces in final)

- Tone: commercial, board-room, narrative-driven
- Lead with: business shape, GTM thesis, fundraising posture
- Don't lead with: product taxonomy or technical architecture
- Expected: questions about scale shape, market sizing, competitive moat, capital efficiency
- Decision criteria: would they sign off on the hire?

## Scope-disambiguation question variants by altitude

These are templates. The skill should customize them with the actual role name + interviewer context.

**Hiring manager (when incumbent product leader exists):**
> "The role description framed this as the first product leader role, and I see the incumbent product leader is already in seat per public profile. Help me understand how the new role relates to that seat. Is it above, beside, or backfilling?"

**Hiring manager (clean scope):**
> "What's the core mandate the company needs this role to deliver in the next 12 months? What would make this hire feel like an obvious yes by the end of year one?"

**Recruiter screen:**
> "What stage of the process are we in - is this an exploratory conversation or are you running a structured search with a comp band already set?"

**Peer VP:**
> "Where do you think the boundary should sit between what I'd own and what you'd own, if I came in? Where would friction show up first?"

**Final / CEO:**
> "What's the highest-stakes product decision you'll face in the next 12 months that you'd want me to own from day one?"

**Working session:**
> Use a probing question on the actual product trade-off in front of you, not a generic scope question.

## Anchors-by-vertical quick reference

When the company is in a specific vertical, lead with the closest user anchor:

| Company vertical | Tier 1 anchor | Tier 2 anchor |
|---|---|---|
| Regulated B2B SaaS (privacy, compliance, GRC) | Northstar multi-jurisdiction | Vector AI-native SaaS |
| FS / payments / fintech | Atlas / Quanta Pay FS rails | Northstar regulated framework |
| Insurance (insurtech, MGA, etc.) | Atlas FS rails as commercial-insurance adjacency | Northstar regulated multi-jurisdiction |
| Healthcare / healthtech | Northstar regulated multi-jurisdiction | Atlas compliance tooling |
| Marketplaces (B2B2C, three-sided) | Lumen B2B2C three-sided | Helix enterprise GTM |
| AI-native scale-up | Vector AI-native SaaS (product layer) + Compass operating model (org layer) | Northstar AI-embedded scale |
| Post-IPO scale-up | Helix IPO readiness + Northstar scale-up shape | Lumen founder-CEO partnership |
| Early-Series-A / Series-B | Helix 6x ARR + Vector builder-mode | Northstar scale-out |
| Crypto / Web3 (use HONEST framing per crypto-native rejection memory) | Atlas FS rails (NOT crypto-native operator) + Vector AI-native | Be explicit about non-crypto-native adjacency |
